"If" : Shisha - Healthy Decorative Lamp

Research

With the help of a multimeter borrowed from the sensor lab, try to find several conductive/semi-conductive materials and/or objects. Trash cans, food vendors, your body, stationery, and parks are good places to start looking... Analyze and document the physical, electrical, structural, perceptual and aesthetic properties for each item found.


Lime : I wanted to know what plants can achieve it. It lead me to discover the lime which not only conducts but also generates electricty according to many youtube videos. The instruction where pretty straight forward indicating a lime can start an LED directly.

After sacrificing a poor lime in the name of science, I discovered that it dind't worked. Actually, the electricity generated from the lime is due to a chemical reaction involving Copper (Cu) and Zinc (Zn).


Salt : By logic, it is made of NaCl where Na is positive and Cl is negative. Thus, it should work in water by connecting to their opposite parts comming from the anode and cathode.


Aluminium : It is conductive by default and I did have a roll of it at home.

Storyboard

Think of different ways you could design a binary interface that utilizes a physical electric switch (i.e. a transistor) as its sole input. This interface must use at least one of the conductive materials/objects that you discovered in your research. Try to imagine a scenario for your binary interface that is not straightforward or too functional. Explain the spatial, temporal and social contexts of your scenario. Describe with a storyboard how the interaction (human -> binary interface) would work.


I was thinking of way to integrate salt with something else to create a certain usage of this element. In the context of our house, I found an old gift from friends in Brazil which was a Shisha. I was thinking that this objects looks great and for some reason I didn't really had it as a decorative object. It was in box in a dirty state.

The latter made me realize that it could be used as a lamp. Moreover, salt has cleaning proprieties against tar residue found after combustion and it has electrical conductivity which can act as a switch indicating that it is time to change the water. It would not only be a lamp but an object enhancing your health by cleaning the water through which you will inhale the next time you smoke.

Caution: Salt can remove cheap painting found in the lower interior of your waterpipe.

Circuit

On the top, the voltage is of 1.5V * 3 = 4.5 V. On the left, there is 3 LEDs in parallel and on the right the switch is the water which will conduct electricity after a little delay when the salt (NaCl) is put inside.

Interface

- Build the interface (on a bread board is fine).
- Bring it to class for a live demo.
- Ensure that it results in a simple electric action.
- Do not use a microcontroller.
